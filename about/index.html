About

My name is Jeffrey Yoo Warren, and I am an artist, community scientist, illustrator, and researcher in Providence, Rhode Island.

I collaboratively create community science projects which decenter dominant culture in environmental knowledge production. I host participatory projects, run workshops and give talks on cultural making, collaborative practice, community science, and open hardware. I am also a contributor to over 150 open source code projects, many of which I started.

Iâ€™m also an illustrator as well as a mentor of programs that support diversity, equity, and inclusion in software engineering, and am Lead Code Community Coordinator at Public Lab as part of this work. (more on this coming soon)

I am a member of AS220 Industries, a board member of the Open Source Hardware Association since 2014, and a board member of alternative education program Parts and Crafts. Jeff holds an MS from MIT and a BA in Architecture from Yale University, and spent much of that time working with artist/technologist Natalie Jeremijenko, building robotic dogs.

Public Lab

In 2010, I co-founded Public Lab, a community science network and non-profit dedicated to democratizing science to address environmental issues that affect people.
